Decarbonization
/ˌdiːˌkɑːrbənɪˈzeɪʃən/
noun
1
詞彙植入 · Vocabulary Anchor
脫碳化
The process of reducing or eliminating carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gas emissions, especially by transitioning away from fossil fuels toward renewable energy sources.
Forum-Ready Sentence
"Our organization has committed to full decarbonization of our operations by 2040, starting with a 50% renewable energy transition this decade."
「我們的組織已承諾在 2040 年前完成營運的全面脫碳化，從這十年將 50% 能源轉換為再生能源開始。」
🎯 論壇常見使用情境
Panel Discussion Opening Remarks Networking Chat Q&A Response Keynote Speech
2
場景短文 · Forum Scenario
🎤
Panel Discussion — Energy Transition
International Sustainability Forum · Q&A Segment
Moderator: "We've heard a lot about net-zero targets today. What does meaningful decarbonization actually look like for a mid-sized company?"

Panelist: "That's the question every CEO is wrestling with right now. True decarbonization isn't just about buying carbon offsets — it's about fundamentally redesigning how you source energy, move products, and even how you work with suppliers. For us, it started with an honest carbon audit. Once you see where your emissions actually come from, the path becomes clearer. The hard part isn't the technology — it's the organizational commitment to stay the course when short-term costs feel painful."

Moderator: "So you're saying decarbonization is as much a culture shift as a technical one?"

Panelist: "Exactly. The companies that will succeed are the ones where the CEO and the frontline worker both understand why this matters."
